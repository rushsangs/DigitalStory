Generation of a Story World From a Linear Narrative

Elevator Pitch: Imagine taking one of your favorite stories and creating new meaningful stories from its context

Group Members:
Daniel Hidalgo
Rushit Sanghrajka
James Kersh
Patrick Chen

Project Description: An algorithm is proposed to generate a story world from a linear narrative. The goal is to extract the actors, affordances, relationships, and the states from the input story and store this our story world structure. Natural language processing is used on the input to produce a more meaningful story world. Once the story has been processed by our algorithm, a story generating algorithm is used to test the range of stories that can be made from our story world structure.

Research Skills:
In [SMITH and WITTEN 1991], a general structure was defined to hold all information on a story world. A connectivity relation called the cango rule was used as a way of enforcing causal links.
In [Meehan 1977], a story generating program called Tale-Spin was designed. It generates a story using a problem solver, an assertion mechanism, and an inference mechanism. The problem solver finds a the next state that brings you closer to the goal, the assertion mechanism saves each state in memory, and the inference mechanism generates the post condition from the event generated from the problem solver.
In [Gervás et al. 2005] a story was generated from a set of inputs using code-based reasoning and then translated to natural text using a natural language generator.
In [Reidl and Leon 2009], an existing story structure was extracted and applied to a new setting.
In [Gervás 2009], a brief introduction to story generating systems was told. Systems like Tale-Spin or Novel Writer were described and analyzed.
